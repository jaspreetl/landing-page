Roses, romance, and rush hour: A data-driven look at NYC on Valentine's Day

MTA Data & Analytics Blog

While working on MTA's Open Data team, I co-authored an article exploring Valentine's Day travel patterns in New York City using MTA Open Data. I worked with datasets from the Socrata API—including MTA Bridges & Tunnels HourlyCrossings and MTA Subway Hourly Ridership—and used DuckDB for querying and Python (Pandas, Seaborn, Matplotlib) for data wrangling and visualization. Through time-series analysis and heatmap comparisons, I examined anomalies, shifts in commuter behavior, and borough-level differences. This project showcases both a technical workflow for handling large public datasets and an applied case study in urban mobility.

SafeScan

As part of a graduate project, my team and I developed SafeScan, a machine learning-powered application that helps consumers evaluate the safety of cosmetic and personal care products. The app uses OCR to extract ingredient lists from uploaded images and applies custom-built models—including linear regression, decision trees, and random forests—to generate a safety score from 0 to 10 based on the presence of potentially harmful chemicals (e.g., parabens, phthalates, sulfates).


We trained and evaluated our models on a Kaggle dataset of 1,472 products, using metrics such as MSE, MAE, and R² to assess performance. The Random Forest model emerged as the most accurate, explaining ~87% of the variance in harm scores.


The final solution was deployed as a Streamlit app, allowing users to upload product images, view extracted ingredients, and receive a transparent safety breakdown. This project bridges technical innovation with public health, offering a scalable, user-friendly tool to promote informed purchasing decisions and encourage safer product formulations.

Dorian

Dorian is a full-stack e-commerce application for specialty coffee. The platform is developed from scratch using the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express, React, Node.js). The customer-facing site allows users to browse products, apply filters, select variants (e.g., size or grind), and manage their cart through to checkout. On the operations side, I created an admin dashboard for inventory and product management. All data — including user profiles, product catalogs, and transactions — is stored in MongoDB, with Express/Node.js powering backend services. The frontend and admin portal are deployed on Vercel, and future iterations will integrate Stripe for secure payments.
